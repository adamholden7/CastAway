The Brule River

The Bois Brule River is one of the Midwest’s most storied trout and steelhead fisheries — a cold, spring-fed river flowing through deep forest before reaching Lake Superior.

From delicate dry-fly fishing in summer to powerful steelhead in the fall and spring, there’s always something happening on the Brule.

  • Wild and stocked trout
  • Steelhead runs (spring & fall)
  • Classic Northwoods scenery

Seasonal Highlights

  • April–May: Spring steelhead & early hatches
  • June–August: Dry flies, terrestrials & evening floats
  • September–November: Fall colors & steelhead

For current conditions and availability, reach out and I’ll let you know what’s fishing best.
